Proof $$ \begin{aligned} \mu_Y &= \mathbb{E}[AX+b] \\ &= A\mathbb{E}[X] + b \\ &= A\mu_X + b \\ \Sigma_Y &= \mathbb{E}[(Y-\bar y)(Y-\bar y)^\top] \\ &= \mathbb{E}[(AX+b - A\mu_X-b)(AX+b - A\mu_X-b)^\top] \\ &= \mathbb{E}[A(X-\mu_X)(X-\mu_X)^\top A^\top] \\ &= A\Sigma_X A^\top \end{aligned} $$
\(Y=AX+b+\epsilon\) Adding a gaussian noise \(\epsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\Sigma_\epsilon)\) gives $$ \begin{aligned} \mu_Y &= A\mu_X + b \\ \Sigma_Y &= A\Sigma_X A^\top + \Sigma_\epsilon \end{aligned} $$
